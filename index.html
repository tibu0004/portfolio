VIGILANT

UX/UI + Code

PROJECT NAME: VIGILANT

CLIENT: CBSA

ROLE: DESIGN LEAD

YEAR: 2020

Allow Border Services Officers (BSOs) to maintain eye contact with travellers while validating the passport details of the person through a voice-activated app, which will then relay the statistics of the traveller back to the BSO.

PAIN POINTS

During an interview, the most important step is to increase the eye contact between the officer and traveller to detect any suspicious behaviour and the current process and time retrieving traveller's information take a long time because the officer has to dislocate to the office to search on multiple databases. So, in this project, the major problems are the time spend in the office and lack of eye contact during the interview.

SOLUTION

The most important feature of Vigilant is being a voice-activated/command app. This functionality expedites the officer's workflow. The app is mainly commanded by the user’s voice but it has an efficient UI to be used as a confirmation of the user’s entries. Additionally, it provides the user with two additional input alternatives: fill the fields by voice manually and typing on the keyboard.

ONE

Research

PRODUCT RESEARCH

The first step of the project was to find similar products available in the market that uses text to speech/ speech to text or voice commands. Since this product is for internal use of CBSA and it was particular, the only products found were mainly translators like iTranslate. But we also got some useful apps like Seeing all witch detects texts and read for visually impaired people and SideChef, a platform of recipes where the user uses voice command to skip the step, move forward or stop the video. This step was essential to get inspiration and find opportunities for innovation.

USER RESEARCH

The user research helped to identify the two main goals of the users during the interviews with the travellers. Accurate information in a shorter time is crucial for them and, while they are validating their passport information, they want to have more eye contact with the traveller to interpret the traveller’s body language. It was also confirmed that their work environment could be busy and loud. When asked them about their concerns about a voice app, they commonly said that some names are difficult to pronounce, and also, the noise can be a trouble to communicate with the app. They emphasized that the BSO’s safety is paramount. Overall, all of them agreed on the idea that the voice app can be helpful.

TWO

Analysis

PERSONA AND SCENARIO

After we gathered information from our users, we could create the personas. Persona is a representation of a group of people who has the same type. It is always helpful to develop personas so that we can focus on our users through a much smaller group of personas. In this phase, we included personality summaries as well as their goals and frustrations about the project. Right after we got the personas, we could create the scenarios and context of use. This way, we could find the pain points and better understand our user's concerns. We could also have an estimative of tasks/steps that the persona would have to perform to reach the goal.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Understanding their environment of work and needs, we could create information architecture. This step is vital to structure and label the content of the app in an effective way. In the prior stage, we had the tasks that each persona performed to reach their goals. The IA comes to merge those tasks and create the flow of the app. This phase was a new challenge because we base our project on full voice controls combined with an interface, so in this project, we had to include path for voice commands and responses, user voice and typing.

THREE

Design

SKETCHES

The sketches were used for directional discussions. This step was essential to understand if the voice control flow was matching with the visual content and screens. We took feedback from our client, and we were able to begin closing in on a design. The sketches were also used to get feedback from our users.

LO FI MOCKUP

We focused on creating the low fidelity mockup to understand the content hierarchy and complicated functionalities for our developers. Lo-Fi is a very effective way to show how the app is going to look like without putting too much effort into the design so that any changes would be easy to change or implement. It also provides enough of a direction for us to communicate with the client.

HI FI MOCKUP

Once we had a solid design direction, we began to produce high fidelity mockups. At this time, I added all the visual content like icons, pictures and real text content. Since we were developing for iOS, we also included Apple standard guidelines for iPhone as well as CBSA colour guide.

FOUR

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

The high fidelity mockups were then converted to an interactive mockup where more screens were added to make the prototype look like an actual app. The mockup was then used to perform iterative rounds of quantitative and qualitative user testing.

You must have XD account to use voice commands

FIVE

Testing

USABILITY TESTING

We performed two rows of usability testing. The first one was presented with the sketches. But this last step, before development, is the most important so we can check with the real users if the app is complying with their needs. I created a script where we went through usability testing, and I had two more people, a note-taker and an observer. I also had a voice-recorder and a computer recording the whole process.

SIX

Code

DEVELOPMENT

As designs were finalized, I began to work with the developers to code them up and maintain quality in execution. A crucial part of this process was that the back end and minimal design were already implemented so that the developers could test the app, and after we performed the user testing and tweaked the final design solutions, I could work with them and check if the interface was matching with the approved prototype.

ALL HEART

UX/UI Design

PROJECT NAME: ALL HEART

CLIENT: ALGONQUIN COLLEGE - UX CLASS

YEAR: 2019

AllHeart is a service provider app to be used as a platform where family member/elderly can search for in-home care service and for health care professionals that are looking for an easy way to provide their services.

PAIN POINTS

Elderly people often struggle to find in-home care and assistance with everyday domestic tasks, self-care, travel to appointments, and other simple activities. It's challenging to know what sort of caregiver to hire for specific tasks, and difficult to know where to look for such a caregiver. Long term care arrangements are more common, making simple one-off needs for support, especially challenging to arrange.

SOLUTION

Create a network for in-home care for elderly people where caregivers can post services, adding necessary details for the elderly and their families to browse and understand them and profile creation for the elderly where would capture their specific needs and requirements

ONE

Research

USER RESEARCH

User Research was done by different interviews with potential users (elderly, relatives, caregivers). These interviews ware essential to understand what the users value the most when looking for a health care professional and what makes an app reliable to them. Especially to the elderly, the main questions were about familiarity with smartphones and what they are looking for the right candidate.

TWO

Analysis

PERSONA AND SCENARIO

For this project, I create two different personas and scenarios. The first was a representation of the users seeking a health care professional, and the other one was representing the caregiver. This way, I could analyze each group of users and define the common points between each side and, as a result, improve the app ideas and content.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Understanding their needs, concerns and desires, I could create the information architecture. This app had to be separated into two sides, the seeker side (list of available professionals) and the caregiver side (manage appointments and accept clients). For both sides, a profile creation is obligatory.

THREE

Design

SKETCHES

Finishing the analysis, I started the design phase, where I create some sketches of how my app was going to look. At this time, I already had all the specifications and must-have for my app, so the critical point was to decide how it was going to be displayed to suit my user needs well. The sketches were also used to perform the first iteration of user testing, and the key screen should have a sequence so the user could perform the steps.

LO FI MOCKUP

With the user testing done, I developed the low fidelity screens. It is a great way to understand the functionality and hierarchy better since you transform the drawings into digital. At this point, I made some changes based on user research and started applying some UI design to understand better how my app was going to look. The best part of creating a lo-fi mockup is that you can change things rapidly since you are not focused on the visual design yet.

HI FI MOCKUP

Once I had a solid design direction, I began working on the high fidelity mockup, including images, icons and real content. The idea of creating these key screens in hi-fi is to focus on the visual design, add details that were missing before and use this as a guideline for the final product.

FOUR

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

Interactive mockups are great for user testing. This is a very meticulous process and takes a considerable amount of time if any changes are needed. At the same time, it gives the user a polished design looking like a final product, so they don't distract or be confused with any unfinished item.

FIVE

Testing

USABILITY TESTING

The final phase of this project was the second iteration of user testing. I tested the functionalities, usability and performance of the app by creating a script where the user had to follow. It is always impressive to perform usability testing because usually, you are so focused on the project that sometimes you have to step back and see from the eyes of the user to find improvements.

ADVENTURE RUN

UX/UI Design

PROJECT NAME: ADVENTURE RUN

CLIENT: ALGONQUIN COLLEGE - UX CLASS

YEAR: 2019

Adventure run is a gamefying fitness app developed especially for Apple Watch which has as objective interact with the user making them more active

GOAL

The goal in this project was to create an easy to use app that runs on Apple Watch where the user can track their progress and collect points/artifacts as well as compete with friends. The inspiration for this app was Indiana Jones, and the idea is to transform the runner into an adventurer. This way, during the run, the user can find some important objects and antiques, making this exercise much more fun. The user can also invite and challenge friends to a treasure hunt, and the person who runs/collect more points wins.

ONE

Design

SKETCHES

For this first step, I used the Crazy 8's process where you have eight pieces of paper, and for each piece, you have about 1 minute to create an interface idea. After that, you analyze the parts and define the best ones. You can also merge to create a better solution. In the end, you sort them and create support pieces to develop your app flow. This is a beneficial process when you have a tight time limit and a simple app.

HI-FI MOCKUP

After I create the low fidelity mockup, I started to develop the visuals, including colours and design the logo. The main part of this app is the icons, so I made sure I was creating something that was matching with the idea as well as with the Apple guidelines. High fidelity mockup is great to understand the app concept and to check if everything is alright before sending the project to develo

TWO

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

From the high fidelity, I developed the interactive mockup. This is a must step and great for user testing. This mockup was used to understand how the user interacts with the app and then define some changes to facilitate the use.

JIA OPTION MAP

UX/UI Design

PROJECT NAME: JIA OPTION MAP

CLIENT: CHEO HOSPITAL

YEAR: 2019

JIA Option Map is a web-based app for teenagers and parents of kids affected by Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis, which shows the best treatments and resources for the disease created by the algorithm.

PAIN POINTS

The client came to us with an ongoing project. The interface was ready, and the mockup code was running for tests. Even though the previous company did the interface, we found gaps in user experience. The path was complicated to understand, and the main problem was that the forms were long and tedious. Since this is an app to be used for teenagers, the critical point is making the app quick to use and interactive. This way, JIA could become an essential resource for their disease.

SOLUTION

I was the UX designer in this project, so my role was to redesign the whole app. Since the main part of the app is the form where the user needs to complete to get the best results, my approach was to break the steps into multiple pages to make the process less annoying. The second approach was to bring a teen identity to the app and make the content less havier so that it could be easier to read.

ONE

Design

VISUAL DESIGN

Since I had a very limited time to work in this project, I skipped the paper sketches and switched directly to the computer. I was able to create good ideas that fit very well with my idea of creating a more juvenile/fun design.

A very important step in the form was to select parts of the body where the user felt pain.

Another step was an infographic to show the steps in the app and a brief description of each step.

All other assets were created based on the main layout so that it could go well with the brand and idea.

HI FI MOCKUP

All the visual content was created along with the hi-fi mockup. Since it was a tight time frame, it was beneficial to jump in front of the computer and design, because I could see the result right away and make changes quickly. The client was very active as well, so it was easy to work in that way so she could see the development almost in real-time.

TWO

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

With the high fidelity, I developed the interactive mockup, which was then used to perform user testing. Additional screens were added to make the process smoother. Make the mockup look like a final product helps the user focus on essential steps without drawing the attention to unfinished designs. Since the project was going further after my internship, the user testing was done internally, and it was proved the efficiency.

THREE

Testing

USABILITY TESTING

Usability testing was an essential step to determine if I was going on the right path. Since the interface created by the previous company was not successful, I had to make sure that the app was beneficial and reliable to the user. The results were a success, and then I passed the project to the next phase: Development.

FOUR

Code

DEVELOPMENT

With the design finalized and approved by the client, I started to work with the developers, making sure that the mockup guidelines were being followed to maintain quality in the execution. I also helped a little bit with CSS. The next phase was to create a test platform to check the efficiency of this app to help those teenagers. The app is still in testing mode.

MIND US

UI Design + Web

PROJECT NAME: MINDUS

CLIENT: ALGONQUIN COLLEGE - BUSINESS CLASS

YEAR: 2020

MindUs is an app that provides free access to reliable resources relating to mental illness while increasing awareness of the difference between normal behaviour and behaviour as a result of poor mental health. The gamified aspect of the application encourages community support and motivates its users to be more physically and socially active.

PROBLEM

Mental health in Canada is an important social problem for individuals, families and communities. Although the federal and provincial governments are well aware of this, and there are several health and social facilities that help people who have mental health problems, the right and timely service still cannot be achieved. Along with the developments in both hardware and software technology, many websites and mobile apps have been created for the purpose of decreasing mental health issues. However, because the demand is continuously growing and the current mental health apps are either not effective, difficult to use, or require payments, mental health still needs a better approach and more effective products such as mobile apps.

SOLUTION

The weak points of the current websites and mobile apps were noticed, and we have developed a new mobile app for people suffering from mental health issues. With the new approach to the problem, the main app features are developed by taking into account free access, ease of use, privacy, means of motivation, and more importantly providing a common platform for people who need help and the individuals who are interested to learn more about mental health

ONE

Design

HI FI MOCKUP

High fidelity mockup was the right approach for this project since the time was very strict. By doing the designs on the computer, I was able to see the final product and make fast changes. This app was a project for the business class, so all the research was made focused on the business model. After this mockup, I could create the interactive mockup.
