VIGILANT

UX/UI + Code

PROJECT NAME: VIGILANT

CLIENT: CBSA

ROLE: DESIGN LEAD

YEAR: 2020

Allow Border Services Officers (BSOs) to maintain eye contact with travellers while validating the passport details of the person through a voice-activated app which will then relay the statistics of the traveller back to the BSO.

PAIN POINTS

During an interview, the most important step is to increase the eye contact between the officer and traveller to detect any suspicious behaviour and the current process and time retrieving traveller's information take a long time because the officer has to dislocate to the office to search on multiple databases. So, in this project, the major problems are the time spend in the office and lack of eye contact during the interview.

SOLUTION

The most important feature of Vigilant is being a voice-activated/command app. This functionality expedites the officer's workflow. The app is mainly commanded by the user’s voice but it has an efficient UI to be used as a confirmation of the user’s entries. Additionally, it provides the user with two additional input alternatives: fill the fields by voice manually and typing on the keyboard.

ONE

Research

PRODUCT RESEARCH

The first step of the project was to find similar products available in the market that uses text to speech/ speech to text or voice commands. Since this product is for internal use of CBSA and it was very specific, the only products found were mainly translators like iTranslate. but we also got some very useful app like Seeing all witch detects texts and read for visually impaired people and SideChef, a platform of recipes where the user uses voice command to skip the step, move forward or stop the video. This step was very important to get inspiration and find opportunities for innovation.

USER RESEARCH

The user research helped to identify the two main goals of the users during the interviews with the travellers. Accurate information in a shorter time is crucial for them and, while they are validating their passport information, they want to have more eye contact with the traveller to interpret the traveller’s body language. It was also confirmed that their work environment can be busy and loud. When asked them about their concerns about a voice app, they commonly said that some names are difficult to pronounce, and also the noise can be a trouble to communicate with the app. They emphasized that the BSO’s safety is paramount. Overall, all of them agreed on the idea that the voice app can definitely be helpful.

TWO

Analysis

PERSONA AND SCENARIO

After we gathered information from our users we could create the personas. Persona is a representation of a group of people who has the same type. It is always helpful to create personas, so we can focus on our users through a much smaller group of personas. In this phase, we could include a summary of their personalities and data as well as their goals and frustrations about the project to be created. Right after we got the personas we could create the scenarios and context of use. This way, we could find the pain points and better understand our user's concerns. We could also have an estimative of tasks/steps that the persona would have to perform to reach the goal.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Understanding their environment of work and needs we could create information architecture. This step is important to structure and label the content of the app in an effective way. In the step before we had the tasks that each persona performed to reach their goals. The IA comes to merge those tasks and create the flow of the app. This phase was a new challenge because our project was based on full voice controls combined with an interface so in this project we had to include path for voice commands and responses, user voice and typing.

THREE

Design

SKETCHES

The sketches were used for directional discussions. this step was important to understand if the voice control flow was matching with the visual content and screens. We took feedback from our client and we were able to begin closing in on a design. The sketches were also used to get feedback from our users.

LO FI MOCKUP

We focused on creating the low fidelity mockup to understand the content hierarchy and difficult functionalities for our developers. Lo-Fi is a very effective way to show how the app is going to look like without putting too much effort into the design so that any changes would be easy to change or implement it also provide enough of a direction for us to communicate with our client.

HI FI MOCKUP

Once we had a solid design direction, we began to produce high fidelity mockups, at this time all the visual content was added as icons, pictures and real text content. Since we were developing for iOS we also included Apple standard guidelines for iPhone as well as CBSA colour guide.

FOUR

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

The high fidelity mockups were then converted to an interactive mockup where more screens were added to make the prototype look like an actual app. This was then used to perform iterative rounds of quantitative and qualitative user testing.

FIVE

Testing

USABILITY TESTING

We performed two rows of usability testing. The first one was performed with the sketches. But this last step, before development, is the most important so we can check with the real users if the app is complying with their needs. I created a script where we went through usability testing and I had two more people, a note-taker and an observer. I also had a voice-recorder and a computer recording the whole process.

SIX

Code

DEVELOPMENT

As designs were finalized, I began to work with the developers to code them up and maintain quality in execution. A key part of this process was that the back end and minimal design were already implemented so that the developers could test the app and after we performed the user testing and tweaked the final design solutions I could work with them and check if the interface was matching with the approved prototype.

ALL HEART

UX/UI Design

PROJECT NAME: ALL HEART

CLIENT: ALGONQUIN COLLEGE - UX CLASS

YEAR: 2019

AllHeart is a service provider app to be used as a platform where family member/elderly can search for in-home care service and for health care professionals that are looking for an easy way to provide their services.

PAIN POINTS

Elderly people often struggle to find in-home care and assistance with everyday domestic tasks, self-care, travel to appointments, and other simple activities. It's difficult to know what sort of caregiver to hire for specific tasks, and difficult to know where to look for such a caregiver. Long term care arrangements are more common, making simple one-off needs for support especially difficult to arrange.

SOLUTION

Create a network for in-home care for elderly people where caregivers can post services, adding necessary details for elderly people and their families to browse and understand them and profile creation for the elderly where would capture their specific needs and requirements

ONE

Research

USER RESEARCH

User Research was made by different interviews with potential users (elderly, relatives, caregivers). These interviews ware important to understand what the users value the most when looking for a health care professional and what makes an app reliable to them. Especially to the elderly, the main questions were about familiarity with smartphones and what they are looking for a good candidate.

TWO

Analysis

PERSONA AND SCENARIO

For this project I create two different personas and for each one a scenario as well. The first was a representation of the users seeking a health care professional and the other one was representing the caregiver. This way I could analyze each group of users and define the common points between each side and as a result improve the app ideas and content.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Understanding their needs, concerns and desires I could create the information architecture. This app had to be separated into two sides, the seeker side (list of available professionals) and the caregiver side (manage appointments and accept clients) for both sides a profile creation is obligatory.

THREE

Design

SKETCHES

Finishing the analysis, I could start the design phase where I create some sketches of how my app was going to look. At this time I already had all the specifications and must-have for my app so the key point was to decide how it was going to be displayed to well suit my user needs. The sketches were also used to perform the first iteration of user testing and the key screen should have a sequence so the user could perform the steps.

LO FI MOCKUP

With the user testing done I could design the low fidelity screens. It is a great way to understand better the functionality and hierarchy since you transform the drawings into digital. At this point I made some changes based on user research and started applying some UI design to better understand how my app was going to look. The best part of creating a lo-fi mockup is that you can change things rapidly since you are not focused on the visual design yet.

HI FI MOCKUP

Once I had a solid design direction I began working on the high fidelity mockup including images, icons and real content. The idea of creating these key screens in hi-fi is to focus on the visual design add details that were missing before and use this as a guideline for the final product.

FOUR

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

Interactive mockups are great for user testing. This is a very meticulous process and takes a considerable amount of time if any changes are needed. At the same time it gives the user a polished design looking like a final product so they don't distract or be confused with any unfinished item.

FIVE

Testing

USABILITY TESTING

The final phase of this project was the second iteration of user testing. I tested the functionalities, usability and performance of the app by creating a script where the user had to follow. It is always impressive to perform usability testing because usually, you are so focused on the project that sometimes you have to step back and see from the eyes of the user to find improvements.

ADVENTURE RUN

UX/UI Design

PROJECT NAME: ADVENTURE RUN

CLIENT: ALGONQUIN COLLEGE - UX CLASS

YEAR: 2019

Adventure run is a gamefying fitness app developed especially for Apple Watch which has as objective interact with the user making them more active

GOAL

The goal in this project was to create an easy to use app that runs on Apple Watch where the user can track their progress and collect points/artifacts as well as compete with friends. The inspiration for this app was Indiana Jones and the idea is to transform the runner into an adventurer, this way during the run the user can find some important objects and antiques making this exercise much more fun. The user can also invite and challenge friends to a treasure hunt, and the person who runs/collect more points wins.

ONE

Design

SKETCHES

For this first step, I used the Crazy 8's process where you have 8 pieces of paper and for each piece, you have about 1 minute to create an interface idea. After that, you analyze the pieces and define the best ones, you can also merge to create a better solution. In the end, you sort them and create support pieces to create your app flow. This is a very useful process when you have a tight time limit and simple app.

HI-FI MOCKUP

After I create the low fidelity mockup, I started to develop the visuals including colours and design the logo. The main part of this app is the icons, so I made sure I was designing something that was matching with the idea as well as with the Apple guidelines. High fidelity mockup is great to understand the app concept and to check if everything is alright before sending the project to development.

TWO

Prototype

INTERACTIVE MOCKUP

From the high fidelity I developed the interactive mockup. This is a must step and great for user testing. This mockup was used to understand how the user interacts with the app and then define some changes to facilitate the use.

INCUBATOR13

Logo + Motion

PROJECT NAME: INCUBATOR 13

CLIENT: RIDEAU ROCKLIFFE
COMMUNITY RESOURCE CENTRE

ROLE: DESIGNER

YEAR: 2019

Incubator13 is an inclusive and accessible (co-working) space located in eastern Ottawa which combines Entrepreneurship and Employment Skills Training, access to co-working facilities and regular events & workshops.

PAIN POINTS

The community center came to us with this new idea of opening a space inside a closed school to help and highlight new entrepreneurs around the Rideau Rockliffe neighbourhood. This idea was in a very early stage and they were working on the space to afford an incubator. Their ideas were so great but a very important piece was missing. How will they make people know about this place and how they will share their workshops and special events? What is the name again?

SOLUTION

The solution was simple, but at the same time, we had to put a great effort to make it work. We team up with multidisciplinary people to create a unique brand and make the website simple but effective. While my teammates were working on content and development on the website I was designated to create the logo.

ONE

Ideas

VARIATIONS

After we defined the name, I started drafting some ideas related to what space was about. Incubator13 is a space to open your mind and make you think outside the box, this way the young entrepreneurs can lunch a very unique business. Since Incubator13 is a RR initiative my idea was to use the same colour palette because linking these two organizations would help people rely on this new space.

TWO

Meanings

THREE

Motion

ANIMATION

Once the logo was done, I started to work on the animation to include on their website land page. The idea was just to create a simple motion graphics to draw attention to the logo and as a result, consolidate the brand.
